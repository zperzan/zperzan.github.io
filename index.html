Environmental Geochemistry Group

We study the ways in which extreme droughts and floods impact groundwater quantity and quality.

Overview

Welcome to the Environmental Geochemistry Group at the University of Nevada, Las Vegas. We strive to understand the fundamental processes that control nutrient and contaminant cycling within the subsurface. To address these questions, we pair numerical models with novel data streams — geophysical surveys and custom-built in situ sensors — and analyze the results using a suite of data science and machine learning techniques. Read more about research here.

The group is led by Zach Perzan, an Assistant Professor in Water Resources within the Department of Geoscience at the University of Nevada, Las Vegas.

We are actively recruiting and hiring research group members at all levels! If you are interested in joining the group, please read more information here.
